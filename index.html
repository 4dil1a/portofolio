About

Where systems
meet curiosity

I'm a fresh graduate in Computer Engineering from Diponegoro University, Semarang, with a 3.83 GPA. My work lives at the intersection of hardware, data, and careful thinking.

My undergraduate thesis involved engineering a precision industrial monitoring system — working with Modbus RTU/RS485 protocols to achieve near-perfect reliability across thousands of communication cycles. I care about systems that actually hold up.

Beyond hardware, I'm currently self-directing into signal processing and neuroscience, exploring how ERP patterns in EEG data might reveal emotional states that people can't or don't express. It's slow, patient work — and I enjoy that.

"I’m deeply drawn to EEG and data because I believe the human body tells stories we aren't always aware of. There's something profoundly honest about raw brain signals—I want to build the systems and tools that learn how to listen to them."

IoT · Industrial

IoT Industrial Furnace Monitoring System

Undergraduate Thesis
► View Implementation

Engineered a real-time temperature and pressure monitoring system for a laboratory furnace. Integrated Arduino Uno R4 WiFi with Autonics TK4S-T4CN via Modbus RTU/RS485. Stress-tested across 12 cumulative hours in industrial conditions.

Achieved 100% reliability across 41,879 communication cycles — with a deterministic 106.31ms latency and an ultra-low 0.073ms jitter.
